Introduction

In this project, we re-analyze the results obtained by Baron et al.Â in their article " Purpureocillium lilacinum and Metarhizium marquandii as plant growth-promoting fungi." using RStudio. The authors test the performances of different fungi obtained from a soil sample while being used as plant growth-promoting organisms.

First, their IAA and P solubilization is tested in order to address the more appropriate strains to be tested in green house experiment.

After that, the second part consists in the test of plan-growth related parameters in presence of the strains chosen in the first part.

The packages used are the next:

Objectives

  1. Redo the statistical analysis from the article with R instead of AGROESTAT.
  1. F and Tukey test for the stain P and IAA values
  2. F and Duncan test for the Greenhouse data
  1. Carry out a Principal Components Analysis (PCA) in order to characterize the data.
  2. Find correlations between fungi strain and plant species.

Part two: Biochemical parametres testing

The Idol Acetic Acid (IAA) produced and the Phosphorous solubilized by each strain was tested in order to choose the ones with the highest amounts. In the article an F and Tukey tests were done to figure this out. Nevertheless, although the IAA data was homeoscedastic, when the residuals of the IAA concentrations were tested for normality, it was found that they do not follow a normal distribution and thus the F and Tukey tests could not be conducted if a data transformation process wasnât made first.
